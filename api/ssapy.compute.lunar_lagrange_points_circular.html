lunar_lagrange_points_circularÔÉÅ

ssapy.compute.lunar_lagrange_points_circular(t)[source][source]ÔÉÅ

Calculate the positions of the lunar Lagrange points in the GCRF frame for a given time.

This function calculates the positions of the five Lagrange points (L1, L2, L3, L4, and L5) in the Earth-Moon system at a specific time t. It accounts for the rotation of the Moon‚Äôs orbit around the Earth, providing the positions in a circular approximation of the Earth-Moon system.

Parameters:ÔÉÅ

tTime

The time at which to calculate the Lagrange points. The position of the Moon at this time is used to compute the Lagrange points.

Returns:ÔÉÅ

dict

A dictionary containing the coordinates of the five Lagrange points: - ‚ÄúL1‚Äù: Position of the first Lagrange point between the Earth and the Moon. - ‚ÄúL2‚Äù: Position of the second Lagrange point beyond the Moon. - ‚ÄúL3‚Äù: Position of the third Lagrange point directly opposite the Moon, relative to the Earth. - ‚ÄúL4‚Äù: Position of the fourth Lagrange point, forming an equilateral triangle with the Earth and Moon. - ‚ÄúL5‚Äù: Position of the fifth Lagrange point, forming an equilateral triangle with the Earth and Moon, but on the opposite side.

Notes:ÔÉÅ

  • The function assumes a circular orbit for the Moon and uses a rotation matrix to align the z-axis with the Moon‚Äôs normal vector.

  • The positions of L4 and L5 are calculated using a rotation matrix to align with the Moon‚Äôs orientation.

  • The gravitational parameters of the Earth and Moon are denoted as EARTH_MU and MOON_MU, respectively.

Example usage:ÔÉÅ

>>> t = Time("2024-01-01")
>>> lagrange_points = lunar_lagrange_points_circular(t)
>>> lagrange_points["L1"]
array([1.02e6, 0.0, 0.0])
>>> lagrange_points["L4"]
array([1.5e6, 1.5e6, 0.0])
